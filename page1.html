HISTORY OF LOURDES COLLEGE

1928

Lourdes College had its humble beginning as a San Agustin Parochial School for elementary boys and girls, founded in 1928 by the late Archbishop James Hayes, S. J. In 1933, the school produced its first twenty (20) graduates in the elementary grades. To respond to a growing need for a high school Catholic education in the city, the school operated with separate principals for the boys and the girls. The girls' division saw the birth of Lourdes Academy managed by the Religious of the Virgin Mary (RVM) Sisters. Sister Ma. Andrea Montejo, RVM was the first Directress and Sister Ma. Esperanza Edralin, RVM was the first Principal. The boys' division, now Xavier University, was managed by the Jesuit priests.

1938

In 1938, Lourdes Academy became Lourdes Junior College, offering Junior Normal and Home Economics Courses. On May 2, 1939, the new buildings were reduced to ashes. In 1945, the school resumed operation under the leadership of Sister Ma. Esperanza Edralin, RVM, with the untiring support of Bishop Hayes. In 1947, it was raised to the status of a College when it offered the Teacher Education Program. The school, then, became Lourdes College.

1950

On July 1, 1950, the Bachelor of Secondary Education (BSE) was given government recognition. The following year, July 1 of 1951, three courses were recognized by the government namely: Two-Year Teacher’s Course in Piano, Two-Year Course in Piano, and Preparatory Course in Music. In 1952, the two-storey building along Real St. (now Capistrano St.) was constructed. Another floor was added the following year. On June 1, 1955, the government gave recognition to the school to offer Bachelor of Music (BM) and Bachelor of Science in Home Economics. The following year, July 11, 1956, the course Bachelor of Science in Elementary Education (BSEED) was also recognized by the government.

1958

On May 9, 1958, the Teacher Course in Music and the Two-Year Secretarial Course (JSC) were granted government recognition and on June 9, 1958, the Four-Year Liberal Arts (AB) was recognized. On June 13, 1960, the One-Year Home Arts (HA) was given government recognition. In 1965, the seven-storey Library and Science building, the first high-rise building in the city, was constructed. This edifice became one of the famous landmarks of Cagayan de Oro City. The construction was supervised by the RVM Congregation’s architect, S. Ma. Mauricia Y. Tapang, RVM.

1970

In 1970, the Grade School level was relocated in Macasandig, Cagayan de Oro City, sharing a portion of the building with Xavier University (Ateneo de Cagayan) Grade School.

1971

In 1971, Lourdes College offered a four-year course in Secretarial Science. Three years after (1974), the College was chosen as a Teacher Training Institute by the Philippine Applied Nutrition Program (PANP) funded by UNICEF through the initiative of the late S. Ma. Elsa Javier, RVM. Its purpose was to serve as the nutrition training and information center of PANP in Region 10.

1976

In 1976, S. Ma. Lourdes Palacio, RVM, Directress, initiated the simultaneous accreditation of the school in three levels. A self-survey was conducted using the guidelines of the Philippine Accrediting Association of Schools, Colleges, and Universities (PAASCU).

1977

On June 14, 1977, the Kindergarten level was given government recognition.

In the school year 1977-78, the first PAASCU Preliminary Surveys were conducted in three departments. The High School had its visit on November 15-17, 1977; the College on January 16-17, 1978; and the Grade School level on January 23-25, 1978 and the following year 1979, Lourdes College got its first stamp of academic excellence when the three departments were granted a three-year accredited status.

1980

In 1980, Lourdes College offered a Bachelor of Science in Food & Nutrition and a Bachelor of Science in Hotel and Restaurant Management, and on June 3, 1981, the Two-Year Cafeteria Management was given government recognition.

1982

In 1982, the school offered the Master of Arts in Home Economics, and in the same year, a five-year re-accredited status was granted to Grade School and High School levels, and a seven-year accredited status was granted to the Education and Liberal Arts Programs by PAASCU.

The construction of the High School Building in Macasandig Campus started and it was first occupied in 1984.

1985

On June 17, 1985, the four-year degree courses were given government recognition namely Hotel & Restaurant Management (BSHRM), Commerce (CM), Nutrition & Dietetics (BSND), and Commerce (BSC) Major in Accounting.

1987

In 1987, a Master of Arts in Education major in Elementary Education was offered.

In 1987-88, the RVM’s commitment to preferential love for the poor gave birth to the establishment of the Mother Ignacia Social Concern Center, Inc. (MISCCI) as the school’s Official Community Service Arm. The Mother Ignacia Night High School was opened to accommodate daughters of economically deprived families and adults who work for their livelihood during the daytime. A project called Buhay: Mother Ignacia Development Project funded by MISEREOR (The German Catholic Bishops’ Organization for Development Cooperation) was established which consisted of income-generating projects, basic and adult education, and provision of a water system. It was participated in by a total of 120 households. As part of this project, the Mother Ignacia School was established in Lower Dagong, Carmen. After ten years, as in any community organizing effort, this school was turned over by Lourdes College to the empowered members of the community for them to become more independent. The school is presently managed by the Department of Education.

1991

In 1991, the Mother Ignacia Housing Project was instituted with the construction of subsidized core houses made of compressed earth blocks and fiber and cement tiles. This was jointly funded by MISEREOR and Lourdes College. Some 110 housing units were constructed and these benefited scavenging families. A very significant component of the project was the economic capability building like income-generating activities, adult literacy programs together with community organizing. The project that Lourdes College had with MISEREOR terminated after three years. In 1994, MISCCI, representing Mindanao, received the National Award for Community Service (NACS) from the Rotary Club of Makati and San Miguel Corporation, with a Plaque of Appreciation and a prize of P70,000.00. The project likewise became one of the showcases of the Asian Regional Consultation for Habitat. The housing project was nominated as one of the Habitat entries for Asia in April 1996.

1992

In School Year 1992-93, two major areas of specialization were added to the Teacher Education Program, namely: Early Childhood Education for Bachelor in Elementary Education and Religious Education for Bachelor of Secondary Education.

1994

In 1994, PAASCU re-visit was conducted for the Grade School and High School levels, and the two departments were granted Level II Accreditation status.

The Kindergarten Building was constructed in 1994 to accommodate the growing population of the Pre-school Program.

During that same year (1994), the school offered a separate Secondary Science Curriculum (SSC) aside from the National Secondary Education Curriculum (NSEC). This was to provide special college preparation to high school students who would like to pursue Science and Math allied courses. The Science Laboratory, which was completed in 1996, was provided to complement the research activities of the SSC students. In the same year, the Gymnasium was constructed to provide a venue for the co-curricular activities of the students.

The Grade School Department was awarded by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports (DECS) as the “Most Effective Private Elementary School” in Region 10 in the school year 1993-94. Likewise, the High School Department was awarded by the DECS as the “Most Outstanding Private Secondary School” in Region 10 in 1994-95, and again in 1996-97, the High School Department was awarded as the “Most Effective Secondary School” in Region 10.

1995

To respond to the needs of society, the school expanded its course offerings in the college. Thus, in 1995-96, three undergraduate courses were opened, namely: Bachelor of Science in Social Work, Bachelor of Science in Accountancy, Bachelor of Library and Information Science, and an additional major in Liberal Arts: Mass Communication. Likewise, the Graduate School opened Master of Arts in Education major in Educational Management and the succeeding year, Master of Arts in Human Resource Development.

1998

Keeping abreast with the trends in technology, the school homepage was launched as part of the world wide web on the internet on February 11, 1998.  Local area networking in computer laboratories and offices of both campuses was installed.  Computerized cataloging was also adopted in the school’s library making it one among the advanced learning resource centers not only in Cagayan de Oro City but even in Region 10 during that time. 

1999

The Basic Education Department was revisited by PAASCU on February 25-26, 1999 for High School and March 1-2, 1999 for Grade School, and were granted another five (5) years of re-accredited status. Sustaining parallel determination, the College had another PAASCU Re-Visit in November 2000 for Teacher Education and Liberal Arts and a Preliminary Visit for Commerce.

2000

In 2000, Lourdes College improved her enrolment system through a customized computer program for the Registrar and the Finance Offices. Likewise, the Learning Resources Center continues to develop library automation using a licensed program to improve bibliographic accessibility and computer-assisted research services.

2001

The Social Work produced its first topnotcher in the Social Work Licensure Examination in 2001. During this school year 2001-2002, two additional major specializations in Master of Arts in Education were offered, namely: Childhood Education and Teaching English Communication Arts.

2003

In 2003, two graduates of the Social Work program became topnotchers in the Licensure Examination. One got the 3rd rank and the other the 9th rank. In the same year, the school admitted its first enrollees in Bachelor of Science in Nursing, and the College was conferred the Deregulated Status by the Commission on Higher Education for maintaining a tradition of integrity and excellence.

During the SY 2003-2004, an overpass was constructed and blessed on March 6, 2004, to link the main building in the higher education department with the auditorium, chapel, mini hotel, RVM dormitory, and other facilities located across Capistrano Street.

2004

In the Basic Education Department, the St. Joseph Building was constructed in 2004 which housed the Guidance Office, Computer laboratory, TLE laboratory, Elementary School library, AV Center, and canteen.

2005

On February 21-22, 2005, the PAASCU team visited the High School and Grade School levels. Both were granted Level II accreditation status but the High School level was required to be subjected to an interim visit in the Instruction area after three (3) years.

On August 22-23, 2005, the Teacher Education, Liberal Arts, and Commerce were granted a Level II re-accreditation status for another five (5) years, the Hotel and Restaurant Management Program had its preliminary visit and Social Work Program was granted Level I Accreditation status.

In the school year 2005-2006, Teaching Physical Education was offered as an additional major/field of specialization in the Master of Arts in Education (MAEd).

Considering the growing number of students in the Bachelor of Science in Hotel and Restaurant Management (BSHRM) as well as its Associate course (AHRM), the school created an academic program for this course, separating it from the Business and Accountancy Program during the second semester of School Year 2005–2006. The new structure enabled the dean to provide adequate supervision and monitoring for the development of the students and faculty.

2006

To further provide facilities for the growing population of the HRM Program, the school established the HRM Training Center which was blessed on November 29, 2006. Since its operation, it has provided an avenue for students to hone their skills in hotel and restaurant operations.

In 2006, Lourdes College was designated as a Learning Center for the University of the Philippines Open University or UPOU. The Memorandum of Agreement was signed in September and after the formal assignment in October of the same year, the school began its services to the students through distance learning. The service ended in SY 2012-2013 because the contract was not renewed for the following school year.

Lourdes College graduated its first batch of Bachelor of Science in Nursing in 2006 and obtained an 83% passing percentage in the Nursing Licensure Exam held in December 2006. The Social Work Batch 2007 graduates all passed the Licensure Examination.

2007

In School Year 2007-2008, CHED and TESDA granted the school a certificate to operate the BSHRM Ladderized Degree Program.

2008

On May 9, 2008, the Teacher Education, Arts & Sciences, and Commerce programs earned the Level III accreditation status.

2009

On June 30, 2009, the Social Work program received the PAASCU Level II accreditation status for five years. On September 15, 2009, the High School level had an interim visit by PAASCU.

The school undertook major improvements in its physical infrastructures in support of classroom instruction. In the main building, improvements in the physical plant included the renovation of the speech laboratory (2008), installation of water sprinkler (December 2009), the installation of the elevator, renovation of the college faculty room, and AVR. In the Basic Education campus in Macasandig, the High School Chemistry Laboratory and the Multi-Purpose Hall were likewise improved.

In 2009-2010, the BSHRM program intensified its international internship which was initially done in Singapore in SY 2004-2005 by fielding students in Qatar and four (4) areas in the United States.

2010

After the PAASCU team’s visit to the school last September 27-28, 2010, the college was granted 5 yeas re-accreditation status on the following programs: Level III for Teacher Education, Arts and Sciences and Commerce programs; Level II for Social Work; and Level I for the HRM program. The Nursing program also passed the preliminary survey.

The Basic Education Department was granted Level II re-accreditation status after their November 22-23, 2010 visit. The school was the first institution in the city granted Integrated Basic Education accreditation.

Concerning course offerings in the tertiary level, Master in Library Information Science (MLIS) was offered in the second semester of SY 2010-2011, and Bachelor of Information Technology (BSIT) was opened for the college students starting SY 2011-2012.

Some indicators of competence include the 100% passing rate of Social Work Graduates Batch 2010 and a Nursing graduate who became the 6th placer in the July 2011 Nursing Licensure Examination.

2011

Providing support to quality instruction, the physical facilities continued to be upgraded. In 2011, renovations focused on the music room, physics and anatomy laboratories, HRM faculty room, Mini Hotel, and some offices in the main building. In the second semester of 2011-12, some classrooms were air-conditioned; the auditorium was repainted and made air-conditioned. Renovations were made in the Mother Ignacia building in the Macasandig campus because it was reduced to ashes. Improvements were likewise made in the Library of the main campus, adopting the term “Learning Commons” for this learning resource center.

2012

The acceptance of Nursing freshmen was suspended for 2 years, starting SY 2012-2013 due to a decline in enrolment, and its implications on the needed human resource requirements.

2013

In 2013-2014, the courses under Master of Arts in Education in the graduate school level underwent a preliminary visit by the Philippine Accrediting Association of Schools, Colleges, and Universities (PAASCU) and was granted candidate status for accreditation. In the same year, the school offered a Bachelor of Science in Accounting Technology and a Bachelor of Science in Pharmacy.

2014

During the school year 2014-2015, the Nursing Program resumed the acceptance of first-year students which was frozen two years before. In this same year, the Nursing, Pharmacy and Nutrition, and Dietetics programs are clustered under the Allied Health Program in the Higher Education Department.

In 2014, the following accomplishments were done in the library: 1) expansion of the learning commons Internet Zone providing more internet-connected computer units (from 14 to 27 units); 2) provision of a unique, spacious, and comfortable carrel for individual research/study with 27 seats; 3) provision of Proquest Online database; 4) coffee shop in one part of the learning commons made more functional. Such effort was rewarded by the award granted to the school’s Library as the 2014 Outstanding Academic and Research Library by the Philippine Association of Academic Research Librarians (PAARL).

Improvements done in the Basic Education Department included the offering of the Lourdes College (Mother Ignacia) Open High School to students who can work during the week since classes are only held every Saturday. Instruction was complemented using the blended approach consisting of a combination of online learning and printed modules.

In 2014-2015, the college faculty were re-oriented to the RVM Pedagogy. This serves as an innovative curricular framework to make the instructional processes aligned with the current demands of education.

In the same year, PAASCU granted Level II re-accreditation status to the Bachelor of Science in Social Work and Bachelor of Science in Hotel and Restaurant Management programs. The Social Work program has produced several topnotchers through the years, and in recent years, a graduate got the 10th place in 2014; and another in 2015. Based on the performance of first-time takers in the Licensure Examination from SY 2011-2014, Lourdes College got Rank 2 in the whole country in Bachelor of Science in Social Work, Rank 5 in Bachelor in Library and Information Science; Rank 9 in Bachelor of Science in Nutrition and Dietetics; and Rank 13 in the Bachelor in Elementary Education among schools having less than 50 examinees (Source: Finduniversity. ph as of November 2014).

2015

In School-Year 2015-2016, various developments in the physical facilities were undertaken by the school. In the Higher Education Department, the Information Technology Center (ITC) was built with two (2) well-equipped computer laboratories and the provision of a special iMac laboratory. The Psychology and Business laboratories were likewise provided as well as the newly furnished and more spacious faculty room, students’ consultation room, and offices of the Mother Ignacia Social Concern and Action (MISCA) center, and the Research, Planning, and Development Office. Fire exit stair made of steel was constructed at the back of the Science building from the 6th floor to the ground. Repainting was done with the fence and the exterior part of the auditorium, dormitory, and the main building, which contributed to the aesthetic ambiance of the school.

Major renovations were made in the Registrar’s and Cashier’s Offices, now facing the quadrangle. The lobby was widened and the prayer room was improved with a glass wall and door facing directly the lobby, signifying a warm welcome to all who enter the school. The Transfiguration statue of Jesus was installed on top of the Information Technology Center and the statues of Mary and Venerable Ignacia were placed in front of the Science building.

To further support instruction, additional air-conditioning units and built-in LCDs were installed in all classrooms.

In the Basic Education Department, remarkable improvements were made in the physical facilities such as the reconstruction of the school fence with the marble signage of the school, the improvement of the gate, construction of parents’ waiting shed, expansion of the canteen, improvement of the drainage system, refurbishing of the comfort rooms, repair, repainting and face-lifting of the gymnasium, and landscaping of the playground. Air-conditioning units were installed in all classrooms, and repainting was done.

In June 2015, the Graduate School opened Master in Business Administration (MBA), and in 2016-2017, it offered Master of Science in Hospitality Management (MSHM).

In July 2015, the school subjected itself to Institutional Sustainability Assessment (ISA) and was visited by a team from CHED, Manila. In August 2015, the graduate school had the PAASCU consultancy visit in preparation for the formal visit.

2016

In 2016-2017, the school received the following certificates from PAASCU: Level III accreditation for the Social Work program (February 3, 2016); Level III re-accreditation for Arts and Sciences, Elementary and Secondary Education, and Business Administration programs good for five years; Level I accreditation for the Nursing Program good for three (3) years; and the Nutrition and Dietetics program was granted the eligibility to apply for a formal survey after only 6 months (May 17, 2016).

In May 2016, Lourdes College was recognized by CHED as a Delivering Higher Education Institution (DHEI) for its graduate programs in Childhood Education, Teaching Communication Arts, Teaching Physical Education, and Master in Library and Information Science (MLIS). Seven (7) faculty members were approved by CHED as scholars in their post-graduate studies under the K to 12 Transition Program.

In SY 2016-2017, Lourdes College offered Senior High School with Accountancy, Business and Management (ABM), Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM), and Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) strands, as well as Technical Vocational Livelihood track with a specialization in Culinary Arts and Information and Communication Technology.

2017

In the same school year, the Integrated Basic Education was granted a five-year re-accreditation status. The BS in Nutrition and Dietetics was given Level I PAASCU accreditation status last April 27, 2017. The Bachelor in Accountancy and Bachelor in Information Technology programs received qualifications for the consultancy visit.

In March 2017, 100% of the first takers among the BS in Nutrition and Dietetics graduates made it in the licensure examination.

Renovations of the physical plant in SY 2016-2017 at the main campus included the canteen, the adjoining student lounge, an additional lecture room adjacent to the technical area in the learning commons, and the elevated grand stairways to pave the way for ambulance and fire trucks during emergencies. The school also refurbished the offices, conference, and case rooms of the Center for Graduate Studies and the faculty room for the college and Senior high school faculty.

In SY 2017-2018, two new courses were offered namely: BS in Tourism Management and Bachelor of Culture and Arts Education and Bachelor of Technology and Livelihood Education.

In the same year, the first Mother Ignacia cup was organized involving the five (5) tertiary schools in the RVM Northern Mindanao cluster.

The school also launched major renovations such as the improvement of the school façade, the construction of the AVR, nine (9) additional classrooms located at the right and left wings of the main building. Renovations also included the conversion of the former AVR into senior high school library, and the former Allied Health offices into Graduate school library. Chairs in the Auditorium were repainted and the Physical Education room was refurbished to cater to the new course offering in this field. Furthermore, the school started the construction of the sports complex in the LC Farm located in Carmen, Cagayan de Oro City. To protect the school community from the rain and the sun, canopies were constructed over the grand staircase and at the sides of the main building.

2018

To further raise the school’s standard of excellence at the international level, Lourdes College pursued the process leading to certification in International Organization for Standardization (ISO) 9001:2015 with TUV Rheinland based in Cologne, Germany. The school was granted the certificate last July 23, 2018, and it is valid until July 22, 2021.

In SY 2018-2019, the following courses were offered: Bachelor of Science in Accounting Information System (BSAIS), Bachelor of Science in Information System (BSIS), and Bachelor of Technology and Livelihood Education (BTLE).

In terms of the graduates’ performance in the board examinations, 100% of the fresh graduates of the nursing program made it to the Nursing Licensure examinations in November 2018 and June 2019. One hundred percent (100%) of the first takers from the Teacher Education program also made it in the Licensure Examination for teachers in the September 2018 examination.

Relative to the physical plant and facilities, in the same year, a portion of the dormitory building was renovated and converted into five (5) classrooms to support instruction; an additional computer laboratory was set up for the senior high school; the tiling of the quadrangle was completed, the turnstile electronic access system was installed in the gate of the main building; and the basketball, volleyball and tennis courts were completed in the sports complex.

2019

In 2019-2020, two graduates from the Social Work program made it to the 8th place in the licensure examination and a graduate in BS Psychology also made it to the 8th place in the Psychometrician board examination. A number of students excelled in competitions including the championship in Kumbira by the Junior and Senior High school students.

For school building and facilities, six (6) air-conditioned classrooms were constructed at the right side of the campus, along Rizal street. The canteen right below the new classrooms was enhanced; and the faculty room, as well as the clinic, were expanded. The school likewise acquired an enterprise grid equipment for data protection and security, purchased 50 updated computer units for junior high school and acquired scantron machines to facilitate efficient checking of examination results.

2020

On January 20, 2020, the Integrated Basic Education department (Elementary and Junior High school levels) was awarded Level III PAASCU accreditation status. In July 2020, the following programs were granted these accreditation status: Level 1 for BSIT Program; Level II for HM and Nursing Programs; and Level III for Social Work Program.

In March 2020, Master of Science in Social Work was granted government recognition. In the same year, the school operation was affected by the global Covid 19 pandemic prompting the school to shift from the face-to-face mode of delivery to online / flexible learning modality. In support to online learning, the school subscribed to G-suite enterprise (Lourdes College being the first school in the country to subscribe), and converted most of its processes to virtual mode of delivery.

2021

In February 2021, the school produced 2 top notchers (No.6 and 8) and Lourdes College was ranked number 2 top performing school nationwide in the Social Work examination. In the same SY, CHED granted recognition for the Bachelor of Culture and Arts Education (BCAED) and Bachelor of Technology and Livelihood Education (BTLED). The school was likewise recognized as a Delivering Higher Education Institution (DHEI) for the SIKAP grant of CHED.

The school was granted ISO re-certification last April 26, 2021. Furthermore, Lourdes College expanded its international efforts by forging partnerships with universities in Vietnam, Indonesia, Malaysia, and SEAMEO; by engaging in conversations with international resource experts about enhancing distance learning, research, innovations in technology and teaching through webinars that are shared with other RVM schools, public and private school teachers, and other members of the community. Students also benefited through their participation in these activities.

2022

On April 28, 2022, TUV Rheinland renewed the ISO certification to Lourdes College after the first surveillance audit. Considering the health status of the community, the school slowly transitions to the new normal by offering limited face-to-face classes for laboratory subjects. Retrofitting was also done to prepare the campus for the eventual in-person limited instructional processes.

PAASCU granted re-accreditation certificates to the following programs: Master of Arts in Education (Level II), Elementary and Secondary Teacher Education; Arts and Sciences; Business Administration (Level III); and Nutrition and Dietetics (Level II) programs after their virtual visit last September 30 to October 1, 2022, and October 13–14, 2022.

In October 2022, the K-10 classes adopted the hybrid flexible learning mode with seven (7) hyflex equipment installed in their classrooms. Four (4) hyflex equipment were also provided in the graduate school and college laboratories to facilitate learning, thesis defense and webinars for both onsite and online learners. The Senior High School, college and graduate school employ the blended learning mode with specific days allotted for onsite and online learning modalities. The school was granted the second recertification by ISO after passing the second surveillance audit last May 11, 2023. Last May 18, 2023, PAASCU granted Level 3 re-accreditation certificate to the Hospitality Management Program which is valid until February, 2025.

Lourdes College continues to provide opportunities for the integral formation of the learners. The graduates are now serving in the different sectors of society – education, business and industry, government service, religious institutions, and others - in the country and foreign lands. Motivated by the Gospel values and the example of Mary and Venerable Ignacia del Espiritu Santo, this RVM Catholic school remains steadfast and zealous in its commitment to serve the community through its education ministry.
